My Journey Into IT and Cybersecurity

I did not begin my career in technology. Before returning to school, I spent more than a decade working in roles that required leadership, problem solving, and responsibility for real-world outcomes.

During that time I became increasingly interested in the systems behind the tools we relied on every day. I wanted to understand how technology works, how systems connect, and why they sometimes fail. That curiosity eventually led me to pursue a degree in Information Technology with a focus on cybersecurity.

Today I am an IT student building practical skills in networking, operating systems, cloud infrastructure, and security fundamentals. I am especially interested in defensive security, threat detection, and understanding how complex systems behave in real environments.

While I am still early in my career in technology, I bring experience from outside the field that shapes how I approach technical problems: patience, accountability, and a willingness to learn continuously.

Learning the Hard Way and Enjoying It

Learning technical skills later in life has been both challenging and rewarding. Many of my classmates have been coding for years, which pushes me to stay disciplined and curious as I build my own foundation.

Progress in this field comes from steady practice, experimentation, and learning from mistakes. That process is part of what makes working in technology interesting.
