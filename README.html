My Journey Into IT and Cybersecurity

I did not start my career in technology. I earned my way here.

I have spent over a decade leading teams, solving real world problems, and being accountable for outcomes in environments where things break loudly and immediately if you get them wrong. Along the way, I realized I was most energized by the systems behind the scenes, how things connect, where they fail, and how to make them more resilient. That curiosity turned into a deliberate career pivot into Information Technology, with a focus on cybersecurity.

Today, I am an IT student and cybersecurity practitioner in training, building skills across networking, systems, cloud infrastructure, and security fundamentals. I am especially interested in threat detection, defensive security, and understanding how complex systems fail under real world conditions, not just in textbooks.

Learning the Hard Way and Enjoying It

Learning to code later in life is humbling. My younger peers seem to absorb new languages the way I absorb coffee, rapidly and in alarming quantities. But that high bar is exactly what motivates me. I bring discipline, persistence, and perspective earned outside the classroom, and I pair it with a relentless drive to close any technical gap in front of me.

Progress over ego. Curiosity over comfort.

If I ever look too confident, it is probably because my code compiled on the first try. That is rare enough to celebrate.

What You Will Find Here

This site is both a portfolio and a living record of growth. You will find hands on projects demonstrating applied IT and cybersecurity concepts, write ups that show how I think through technical problems, and tools and code I have built while learning.

If you are interested in practical security, honest learning curves, and someone who treats reliability and responsibility as non negotiable, you are in the right place.
